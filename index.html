End Cash Issue in India.
Dear Prime Minister, let's simplify common citizen's life.

Mr. PM, Thanks for the demonetization of ₹500 & ₹1000 notes.
We welcome this step.

But, let's take another step and use technology to
make common citizen's life easier in this current Run-To-Bank/ATM (for Cash) disorder or chaos.

  • Why & How?

What needs to be done?

Let's build a distributed information system for citizens and bank branches/ATMs.

Problem we're facing?

Cash is one of biggest daily need of people and economy. Current demonetization move requires people to rush to Banks/ATMs either to deposit or to withdraw money.
There are long waiting queues in Bank branches or ATMs and that requires people to stand idle and wait for several hours (even days) for their turn to transact with bank.
Most people keep waiting in lines and still have no guarantee about getting their transactions done. Citizens are clueless about How much time they need to wait? OR When they should visit back? OR If bank have required resources to do transactions?

Easy solution?

An online website backed by a Ministry or Govt Dept where:

  • People submit their deposit or withdrawal requests on this secure and easy to access website by going to their State/District/Town -> Branch or ATM.
  • They fill a form with details like 'Name, Mobile, Adhaar Card No. or PAN Card No., Amount to Deposit/Change/Withdraw' (Limited to ₹2.5 Lakh or less per day as per Govt rules) and submit.
  • The system should give a unique access token with time to visit bank branch or ATM with required documents/money.
  • The system can SMS unique code or time of visit to citizens on mobile number.
  • Each branch manager gets access to a secure dashboard where he/she can add/edit details like When new Cash is available in respective branch or ATMs OR see people's incoming requests
  • Emergency Cash Feature: Those who need cash in emergency situations like Hospital/Marriage etc. can upload their respective scanned photo of bills or invitation card.

    • Is it possible?

  • Yes, Because we don't need access to any bank database or financial system.
  • Yes, Because such website or system can run indepedently under any ministry or central government department (for example Finance or Telecom Ministry).
  • Yes, Because it's just a facilitator system between Citizens & Bank Branches or ATMs.
  • Yes, Because anyone in Bank branch can manage such a system.
  • Yes, Because common people won't need to waste several human resource hours in standing queues.
  • Yes, Because it will avoid the chaos/confusion, as people would know about when they should visit branch or ATM to withdraw/deposit cash with their unique token.
  • Yes, Because developing and launching such a system is easy and possible.
